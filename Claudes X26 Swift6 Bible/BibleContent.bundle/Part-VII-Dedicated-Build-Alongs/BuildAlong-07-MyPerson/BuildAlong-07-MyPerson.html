Build-Along 07: MyPerson

Status: planning phase as of 2026-04-24. App not yet scaffolded. Walkthrough fills in once v1.0 scaffolds; expanded planning notes below.

MVP concept: a personal contact-relationship manager. Capture new people you meet, track how you met them, set follow-up reminders, and see all your relationships in one focused place. Three ways to capture a new person's contact info — from "hand the other person your phone" all the way to "tap phones together."

What MyPerson will teach

  • Contacts — reading and writing CNContact via CNContactStore. Authorization, search, create, update, delete.
  • ContactsUI — CNContactViewController for the native display/edit surface.
  • NearbyInteraction — peer-to-peer ranging between iPhones using the UWB chip (U1 in iPhone 11+, U2 in iPhone 15 Pro+). This is the framework that powers NameDrop-style "tap to share" behavior.
  • MultipeerConnectivity — fallback peer discovery for devices without UWB hardware (older iPhones, iPads, Macs). Bluetooth + local Wi-Fi.
  • EventKit (secondary) — optional follow-up reminders for "check in with this person in two weeks" flow.
  • Signature layer: MVP feature + Under the Hood view + About & Send Feedback.
  • Distribution: App Store listing — personal-CRM is an established category (Monica, Dex, Clay) and Apple doesn't ship one. Clear product-market fit for the teaching example.

Three ways to capture a new contact

  1. Tap-together share (best) — both iPhones have MyPerson open, bring them close, the UWB ranging triggers a contact-card exchange. Same UX as NameDrop but captured inside the MyPerson database with relationship metadata.
  2. Peer discovery (middle) — if one device doesn't have UWB, fall back to MultipeerConnectivity over Bluetooth. Slower, but works across iPhone / iPad / Mac.
  3. Hand over the phone (fallback) — open a "New Person" form, hand the phone to the other person, they type their info directly. Works with any human, no device pairing required. This is the worst case and still a first-class path.

What differentiates MyPerson from Apple Contacts

  • Relationship context — how you met, relationship type (family / friend / colleague / acquaintance), importance, notes.
  • Last-contacted tracking — cold? warm? haven't reached out in months? Sort by relationship health, not alphabet.
  • Follow-up reminders — "check in with X in two weeks" creates an EventKit reminder; the app surfaces overdue follow-ups on open.
  • Capture-speed ergonomics — Apple Contacts' new-contact flow is slow. MyPerson's "hand over the phone" form is one screen, fill it out, done.
