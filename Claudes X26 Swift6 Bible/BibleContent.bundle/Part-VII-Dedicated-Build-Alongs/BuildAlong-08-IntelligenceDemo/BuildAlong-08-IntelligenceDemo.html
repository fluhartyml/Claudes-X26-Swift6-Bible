Build-Along 08: IntelligenceDemo

Distribution: TestFlight beta. The concept was a camera-driven "what is this?" scanner — point the camera, get object classification (CoreML), extracted text (Vision OCR), language of that text (NaturalLanguage). It would have shipped to the App Store, except Apple already ships this natively.

MVP concept: a privacy-first camera inspector. CoreML image classification + Vision OCR + NaturalLanguage language detection, all on-device.

Why TestFlight, not App Store

Apple Intelligence / Visual Intelligence (iOS 18+, refreshed in iOS 26) already provides a camera-driven "what is this?" experience on every modern iPhone natively. Google Lens has been on iOS for years too. Our version wouldn't add enough differentiation to clear App Store Guideline 2.2 — a reviewer would reasonably say "Apple and Google already do this, why is yours here?" Without a strong differentiator (a specific niche, a unique interaction model, a privacy story that stands out), the honest distribution channel is TestFlight.

The teaching value of CoreML + Vision + NaturalLanguage is still real — those frameworks remain worth learning. The app is still worth building as a teaching artifact. It just ships via TestFlight instead of competing with Apple Intelligence on the store.

What IntelligenceDemo will teach

  • CoreML — loading a bundled .mlmodel or .mlpackage (e.g., MobileNetV2 image classifier), running inference on a CVPixelBuffer from the camera feed.
  • Vision — VNRecognizeTextRequest for OCR, VNClassifyImageRequest for Apple's built-in classifier, combining requests in one VNImageRequestHandler.
  • NaturalLanguage — NLLanguageRecognizer for language detection on extracted text, plus NLTagger for named entities if the extracted text is a sign or label.
  • AVFoundation (supporting) — camera capture session, live preview layer, per-frame sampling into the Vision + CoreML pipeline.
  • Signature layer: MVP + Under the Hood + About & Send Feedback.
  • Distribution: TestFlight — readers get an install link from the appendix/wiki.

Current state

  • No Xcode project yet

Next step

Scaffolded after earlier build-alongs ship; TestFlight distribution only. If a clear differentiator emerges later (e.g., a specialized niche like botanical-identification or historical-coin-classification) the app could be re-scoped toward App Store at that time, but the default direction is TestFlight.
