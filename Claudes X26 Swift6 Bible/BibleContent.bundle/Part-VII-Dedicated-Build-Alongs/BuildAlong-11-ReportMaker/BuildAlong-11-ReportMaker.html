Build-Along 11: ReportMaker

Status: planning phase as of 2026-04-24. App not yet scaffolded. Walkthrough fills in once v1.0 scaffolds; expanded planning notes below.

MVP concept: a business travel expense tracker that collects receipts, categorizes spending (hotel, meals, transport, incidentals), and generates a clean PDF report at trip end. Built for anyone who expenses a business trip and hates manually assembling a reimbursement packet.

What ReportMaker will teach

  • Swift Charts — spending-by-category bar chart, day-over-day line chart, top-expenses summary. All native Swift Charts, exported into the PDF.
  • PDFKit — generate a multi-page expense report PDF: cover page with trip summary, per-day breakdown, category totals, embedded receipt images, signature block. Native PDF assembly, not screenshot-printing.
  • PhotosUI / VisionKit (supporting) — capture receipts via camera or photo library, optional OCR via VisionKit for auto-filling line items.
  • SwiftData (supporting) — persist trips, expenses, receipts between sessions.
  • Signature layer: MVP feature + Under the Hood view + About & Send Feedback.
  • Distribution: App Store listing — business expense tracking is a proven market (Expensify, SAP Concur, FreshBooks); Apple doesn't ship one; clears 2.2 on usefulness.

Core flows

  • Start a trip — trip name, dates, purpose. Creates a new expense container.
  • Add expense — pick category (hotel / meals / transport / other), amount, optional vendor, optional receipt photo. Tap Save, back to trip dashboard.
  • Trip dashboard — spending-by-category chart, day-over-day chart, running total, list of receipts. Snapshot of where the trip money is going.
  • Generate report — one button makes the PDF. Cover page + per-category breakdown + embedded receipts + totals. Share sheet to email, Files, AirDrop.

Why ReportMaker passes App Review on merit

Business expense tracking is an established category with real users and Apple doesn't ship one. A clean, focused PDF-out app backed by Swift Charts and PDFKit is a legitimate product — not a demo. Alternate concept Michael floated: journal export (turning a diary into a clean PDF). Same underlying kit combination, similar App-Store shape, different problem domain.
