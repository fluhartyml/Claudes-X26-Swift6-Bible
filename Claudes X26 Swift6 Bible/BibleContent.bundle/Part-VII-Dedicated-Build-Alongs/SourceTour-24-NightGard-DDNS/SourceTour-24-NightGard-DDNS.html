Source Tour 24: NightGard DDNS (Self-Publishing Case Study)

Status: self-distributed, notarized. Not on the App Store by shape (daemons can't be App Store distributed). Mac utility.

Self-publishing case study. Pairs with NightGard Commander to show two different reasons apps leave the App Store — choice (Commander) vs. necessity (DDNS).

What NightGard DDNS shows

  • LaunchAgent / launchd integration — the app runs as a background service, not a windowed app
  • Persistent network client (keeps a Dynamic DNS record up to date with the user's changing home IP)
  • Keychain storage for service credentials
  • Tiny scope — daemon apps can be very small

Why App Store distribution was never an option

The App Store doesn't accept daemons or LaunchAgents. An always-on background network client that runs outside of a user-facing app window doesn't fit any App Store app category. Readers learn that some app shapes are simply not App Store material, and self-publishing isn't a fallback — it's the only path.
